Abstract

Spacecraft propulsion systems are continuously under development to improve efficiency of fuel and to bring new possibilities of deep space exploration. This paper discusses the differences between current propulsion systems such as chemical and ion and how they have been developed to improve rocket mission efficiency. It also goes through new propulsion ideas that are still in early stages of development and are being researched in order to bring new possibilities to deep space travel therefore showing the future of spacecraft propulsion systems.

Introduction

Over time spacecraft propulsion systems have been looking towards greater fuel efficiency, lighter rockets and deeper space travel. A good example is the cryogenic engine which directly improves the efficiency of chemical propulsion by 30% (Vaidya, 2023) by keeping the fuel at low temperatures. An intriguing idea that is quite recent is the breakthrough Starshot project which would use light to propel micro or even nano scale spacecraft to the nearest star Proxima b in the Alpha Centuri system which is an exciting concept. Firstly, chemical and ion propulsion is discussed along with a comparison of their efficiency and practical use in rockets and then other means of propulsion are considered such as nuclear thermal propulsion, the cryogenic engine and laser propulsion with the possibilities they bring.
